Health & Wellness

"Discover the importance of timely vaccinations for your child's health. Stay informed about essential vaccines, their schedules, and how they protect your little ones from preventable diseases."

Hepatitis B (HepB)

Protects against Hepatitis B, a serious liver infection caused by the Hepatitis B virus.

Hepatitis A (HepA)

Protects against Hepatitis A, a liver infection spread through contaminated food and water.

Rotavirus (RV)

Prevents severe diarrhea and vomiting caused by the rotavirus in infants and young children.

Diphtheria, Tetanus, & Pertussis (DTaP)

Protects against three serious diseases: diphtheria, tetanus (lockjaw), and whooping cough (pertussis).

Haemophilus Influenzae Type B (Hib)

Prevents infections caused by Haemophilus influenzae type B, which can lead to pneumonia, meningitis, and other serious conditions.

Pneumococcal (PCV)

Protects against pneumococcal bacteria, which can cause pneumonia, meningitis, and bloodstream infections.

Inactivated Poliovirus (IPV)

Prevents polio, a viral disease that can cause paralysis and is potentially life-threatening.

Influenza (Flu)

Provides protection against the seasonal flu, a contagious respiratory illness caused by influenza viruses.

Measles, Mumps, & Rubella (MMR)

Protects against three viral infections—measles, mumps, and rubella—that can lead to serious complications.

Varicella (Chickenpox)

Prevents chickenpox, a contagious disease that causes an itchy rash and fever.

Health Tips for Parents

  • Ensure proper nutrition for your child’s growth and development.
  • Encourage regular physical activity and playtime.
  • Get regular check-ups and vaccinations on time.
  • Teach children about hygiene and handwashing.

Vaccination Myths & Facts

There are several myths surrounding vaccinations, but the facts tell a very different story. Let's clear up some common misconceptions:

Myth: Vaccines can cause the disease they are meant to prevent

Fact: Vaccines are designed to teach the immune system how to protect the body from specific diseases. They do not cause the diseases they are meant to prevent.

Myth: Vaccines are not safe

Fact: Vaccines are thoroughly tested for safety and effectiveness before being approved. They go through multiple stages of clinical trials, and only safe vaccines are allowed for public use.

Myth: Natural immunity is better than vaccine-acquired immunity

Fact: Natural immunity can result from getting the disease, but it often comes with risks and complications. Vaccines provide immunity without the dangers of contracting the disease.

Myth: Vaccines are unnecessary if the disease is rare

Fact: Even rare diseases can cause significant harm if contracted. Vaccines help prevent outbreaks and protect vulnerable populations who cannot be vaccinated themselves.
